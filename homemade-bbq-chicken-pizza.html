Some people are super afraid of making their own pizza dough because of a number of reasons:

A. They have “yeast anxiety” – it’s a real thing, people. They may worry that their water isn’t hot enough, it’s too hot, the yeast won’t rise, etc. I have heard it all. I will give you a few tips to help with this yeast dilemma.

B. They think it’s going to take 4 hours to make a pizza. No one wants to spend 4 hours making a pizza! This dough whips up fast, rises in less than an hour and cooks in about 16 minutes. Not too shabby.

C. They think homemade pizzas are bland. Whoa, what? Hold up, now. We can solve this issue. Let’s up the spice. Let’s add flavorful toppings. Let’s bring some heat.

Here are a few tips to ensure success in making homemade pizza dough:

A. Let the yeast rise in a bowl with warm water (approximately 110 degrees – should feel very warm to the touch). Add a touch of sugar because yeast LOVES sugar. Salt is not a friend of yeast so we add the salt after the yeast proofs. Within a few minutes you should see the yeast start to foam and that is how you know it’s working.

B. Let the dough rise to double in size. You want that pizza crust to be light, airy, with a chewy bite to it. Cover it so the outer layer doesn’t get dried out and place in warm spot. If you live in a warm place, put it near a windowsill to expedite the rising process.

C. Bake it at a high heat. If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven as it is preheating to get it nice and hot.

The secret ingredient in the sauce is a touch of ranch dressing to give it some creaminess.  You can use homemade ranch dressing or straight from the bottle. I suggest using spicy bbq sauce because it gives it a burst of flavor.

Homemade Pizza Dough:

BBQ Chicken Pizza:

  • In a small bowl, dissolve your yeast in warm water. Make sure that your water is warm (not too cold, not too hot, approximately 110 degrees F).Add sugar and stir. Let proof for 10 minutes. You should see it start to foam to know it's working.

  • In a separate mixing bowl, combine flour, salt, 1 1/2 T. oil, and water yeast mixture. Add only 2 1/2 cups of bread flour and watch for it to start pulling away from the bowl to know it's ready. If you need more flour, add a 1 Tablespoon at a time until the dough comes together into a solid ball.

  • If the dough is too dry, add additional water, 1 tablespoon at a time. Scrape the dough onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead into a smooth, firm ball.

  • Grease a large bowl with remaining olive oil, add the dough, cover the bowl with plastic wrap and put it in a warm area to let it double in size, about 1 hour. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and roll out into circle. Let it rest for 10 minutes.

  • Preheat oven to 450 degrees.

  • Meanwhile, in large skillet, add olive oil and cook chicken over medium heat for 7-9 minutes or until cooked through. Remove from heat and add lime juice and salt and pepper.

  • In small bowl, combine spicy bbq sauce and ranch dressing.

  • Place pizza crust on round pizza stone or pizza pan. Spread creamy bbq sauce over pizza crust. Top with mozzarella and cheddar cheeses, red onion, and optional jalapeno.

  • Bake for 14-19 minutes, until crust is golden brown and cheese is melted.

  • Top with fresh cilantro.
