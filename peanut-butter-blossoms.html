What are Peanut Butter Blossoms?

Peanut Butter Blossoms are a classic and popular cookie that combines the rich flavors of peanut butter with a sweet and chocolatey center. It is a soft, tender peanut butter cookie topped with a creamy milk chocolate Hershey’s kiss. The key feature of these cookies is the placement of a Hershey Kiss on top. This is one of the most popular Christmas cookies.

Why are they called Peanut Butter Blossoms?

The original recipe for these cookies originated in 1957 in a Pillsbury Bake-Off Competition The cookies were made by contestant Freda Smith, who created this classic cookie recipe.

    What kind of Peanut Butter to Use?

    I suggest using a creamy peanut butter butter like Jif or Skippy’s.

    I do not recommend using natural peanut butter as it doesn’t have sugar in it and you will end up with dry, crumbly cookies.

    Tips for making the Perfect Peanut Butter Blossoms:

  • Quality Ingredients: Start with high-quality ingredients to ensure the best flavor and texture. Use fresh, creamy peanut butter, real butter, and milk chocolate Hershey Kisses. The better the ingredients, the better the final result.
  • Chilled Dough: Refrigerate the cookie dough before shaping it into balls. Chilled dough helps the cookies maintain their shape during baking and prevents them from spreading too much. This step also enhances the texture, resulting in a chewy center.
  • Properly Measure Flour: Accurate measurement of flour is crucial for the success of any cookie recipe. Use the spoon and level method to prevent packing the flour, ensuring the right balance for the perfect Peanut Butter Blossom.
  • Unwrapping Hershey Kisses in Advance: Unwrap the Hershey Kisses ahead of time to streamline the baking process. This allows you to place the chocolates on the warm cookies immediately after they come out of the oven, ensuring they adhere well without melting excessively.
  • Baking Time Awareness: Keep a close eye on the cookies while baking. Peanut Butter Blossoms are best when they have a slightly golden edge but remain soft in the center. Overbaking can lead to a crumbly texture, so set a timer and check for doneness. It is better to underbake these cookies than to overbake.

    • How to make Peanut Butter Blossoms (Peanut Butter Chocolate Kiss Cookies):

    This is the BEST Peanut Butter Blossoms Recipe. I found this recipe in my Grandma’s church cookbook and it has been a cookie we have on repeat in our home during the holidays. 

    It all starts with a soft peanut butter cookie. No hard cookies here! The peanut butter cookie needs to be perfectly chewy and full of peanut butter flavor. The peanut butter cookie dough is rolled into sugar and then baked until soft.

    After the peanut butter cookies are baked, a single Hershey Kiss is placed in the center. This peanut butter chocolate kiss cookie will be one of the stars on your Christmas cookie plate.

  •  Cream butter and sugars for 4 minutes or until light and fluffy. This ensures the texture is perfectly creamy and no graininess from the sugar is left.
  • Add peanut butter, egg, milk, and vanilla and cream for 2 minutes longer, scraping the sides of the bowl often.
  • Stir in flour, baking soda, and salt and mix until just combined. Do NOT overmix as it creates more gluten and will result in a tougher cookie.
  • Roll into 1-inch balls and roll into sugar. Bake just until the cookies are cooked through but still soft and chewy. I like to slightly underbake my cookies.
  • While the cookies are baking, unwrap the Hershey Kisses so you are ready to put them on the hot cookies.  While they are still warm, press a Hershey kiss into each peanut butter cookie.

    • FAQ’s:

    Can you make the dough ahead of time?

    Absolutely! You can make the dough ahead of time, wrap it tightly in plastic wrap, and place in a bag in the refrigerator until you are ready to use. I suggest chilling the dough for 24-48 hours.

     Is there any substitute for Hershey Kisses?

    Since Hershey’s kisses are the star of this cookie, I suggest using them in these Hershey kiss cookies. However, you can use a thick square of milk or dark chocolate instead.

    Why are my Peanut Butter Blossom Cookies dry?

    These are meant to be soft and chewy cookies but there may be two reasons why they turned out dry. The number one reason is overbaking the cookies. Make sure not to overbake these cookies or it will dry them out. The second reason is adding too much flour. This may happen as a result of packing down the flour in the measuring cup which will add too much flour to the cookie dough.

    Why aren’t the Hershey’s kisses sticking to my cookies?


    The Hershey’s kisses need to be gently pressed to the cookies as soon as they come out of the oven. I suggest having the Hershey kisses all unwrapped so you are ready to press them on top of each cookie. The warmth of the cookies will melt the bottom of the chocolate kiss and stick to the cookies.

    How to store Pb Blossoms:

    These pb blossom cookies can be stored in an airtight container for up to 3-4 days. See below for freezing options.

    Can I freeze Hershey Kiss Cookies?

    Yes! You can freeze peanut butter blossom cookies once they have completely cooled, and the Hershey chocolate kiss is solid again. Place them in an airtight container in a single layer and keep them in the freezer for up to 2 to 3 months.

    Can I freeze Peanut Butter Blossom Cookie Dough?

    Absolutely! I suggest rolling the peanut butter cookie dough into balls and then placing on a sheet in a single layer so they don’t stick together. Freeze for 1 to 2 hours. Once they are frozen, you can transfer the balls to a freezer-safe gallon-sized Ziploc bag.

    When ready to bake, remove from the freezer and roll the balls into the sugar. Let come to room temperature, about 20-30 minutes before baking. Bake as directed.
