Jay Sean’s Biography, Ethnicity

The songbird whose genres are hip-hop, soul, R&B, pop, and bhangra are descendants of a Punjabi Sikh family who reside in North West London. Born in Harlesden, Brent, London on March 26, 1979, the soul singer is of British nationality and Indian ethnicity. He was brought up in Southall, West London alongside his brother Naroop Jhooti. His father Sharan Jhooti and mother Bindi Jhooti who immigrated to London from India, their country of origin, gave him the name Kamaljit Singh Jhooti at birth, but the need for a stage name prompted the young singer to adopt the name of Jay Sean.

Jay Sean attended Latymer Upper School where he completed his General Secondary Education Certificate in bright colors, registering excellent marks in Biology, Chemistry and Maths. His rave results motivated the rising star to enroll at Barts and the London School of Medicine and Dentistry at Queen Mary University. However, he then quit in 2003 to join the entertainment world as a singer and songwriter.
