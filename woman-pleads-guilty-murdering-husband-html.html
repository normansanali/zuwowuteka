North Carolina woman pleads guilty to murdering husband

 Posted on September 13, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  1022 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

A North Carolina woman has pleaded guilty to murder-related charges after she killed her husband and a couple when she caught all three of them in bed together.

Crystal Gambino pleaded guilty to one count of manslaughter and two counts of second-degree murder after accepting a plea deal in the case. 

She was sentenced by a judge to serve 23 to 29 years in prison on the murder charges and six to eight years on the manslaughter charge. The sentences will be served one after the other.  

The 43-year-old woman caught her husband, Giovanni James Gambino, 40, in bed at their Alexis home with Geoffrey Gilliland, 39, and Stephanie Sanchez, 33, in February 2016. She admitted to fatally shooting all three victims.

The man said he was loathed to believe Gambino because 'this wouldn't be the first time she had made up stuff before'.

However he felt he needed to report the incident and asked police to check the house.

Upon arrival at the home, police found the three bodies. Local media said all three bodies were in the bedroom and were naked.

Police did not clarify whether the body of Stephanie Sanchez was found in the yard or whether Gambino intended to take it there.

At the time of the crime, Gaston County Police Capt. Curtis Rosselle told the Gaston Gazette: 'These are people that met, had interaction and then came together. And then the third party did not like it and took action.'

One neighbor, who asked not to be named, told WBTV at the time of her murder arrest that she had a tendency to be violent.

The woman said she stopped talking to her in 2015 after Gambino pulled a gun on her during a disagreement.

'I really didn't think she was going to shoot me,' the neighbor told the TV station. 

'I just thought the best thing I needed to do was leave her alone.' 

Another neighbor, Doug Dallinger, said that the noise of gunshots woke him up when the murders happened. 

'It sounded like a sledgehammer. Just a lot of racket,' he said.
