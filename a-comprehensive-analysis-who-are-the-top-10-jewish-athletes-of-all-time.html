Let's explore the remarkable careers of the top 10 Jewish athletes of all time, celebrating their achievements and contributions to the world of sports.


1. Mark Spitz
Mark Spitz, the famed swimmer who won seven gold medals at the 1972 Munich Olympics. It is a testament to the latter factor - Spitz ability across various strokes and meters in relevant distances makes you an all-time great - as he reached a rare peak on Sunday.


2. Hank Greenberg
Hank Greenberg was an all-time great first baseman and outfielder who laid his mark on baseball history during the 1930s and '40s. A man whose profound abilities and faithfulness made him a cultural pride for American Jews.


3. Sandy Koufax
Sandy Koufax, a southpaw pitcher who dominated baseball with the Los Angeles Dodgers during the 1952-1966 season and led his team to Multiple World Series victories while risking it all by refusing to play one of its most crucial games on Yom Kippur.


4. Ryan Braun

Three-time National League All-Star starter Ryan Braun has left an indelible mark on the sport, earning 2007 N.L. Rookie of the Year honors and finishing third in the '08 National League MVP balloting as well.


5. Dara Torres
Dara Torres, the greatest Jewish Olympian speed freak in history took part in five Olympic games over 24 years and won a total of twelve medals.


6. Max Baer
Max Baer, a renowned heavyweight boxer, became a Jewish folk hero when he defeated German Max Schmeling for the heavyweight championship.


7. Aly Raisman
Gymnast Aly Raisman is a two-time Olympian who made history with six Olympic medals, three of which are golds.


8. Amar'e Stoudemire
Amar'e Stoudemire -Stoudemire is a professional basketball player and has been noted to have some Jewish heritage, proud of his roots.


9. Kevin Youkilis
Kevin YoukilisFormer MLB third baseman Kevin Youkilis won three Gold Glove awards and made three All-Star appearances in his career.


10. Gabe Kapler
Gabe Kapler, a former MLB outfielder and an influential figure in baseball as both player and manager.


How to Identify The Best Jewish Athletes


Career accomplishments: A player's performance, plus any Olympic accolades, world records, and championships.


Cultural Impact: How they impacted the Jewish Community and how they symbolized Jewish pride in Sports


The Big Picture: How they fit in with their sport and where that leaves them on a larger sports basis.


In the End

These champions began bright lights in the sporting world, using talent and willpower to represent their heritage with honor. Their historic accomplishments and perseverance have raised the flags of their sports, in addition to being that shining light others can look up to for years or more. While there may be little left to push through the door, as we celebrate their legacies it is still important that Jewish athletes receive attribution on their impact in sports history.
